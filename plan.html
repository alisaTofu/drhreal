The Plan

A slightly evil roadmap
for the future of the show.

This page outlines how the world of Dr. Horrible’s Sing-Along Blog continues to live on: where to watch it, how fans can gather, and what kinds of projects the lab is quietly planning next.

Nothing here counts as a legally binding villain manifesto—just a practical plan for keeping the musical active, singable, and easy to discover.

Phase-based roadmap

Current focus of the lab.

The plan starts with simple goals: keep the show watchable, keep the songs singable, and make sure fans have a clear place online to land when they search for Dr. Horrible.

Streaming & access

Keep the musical available on at least one major digital platform, with clear links from this site so new fans never have to guess where to watch.

  • • Check links regularly and update if platforms change.
  • • Highlight rental / purchase options side by side.

Sing-along events

Encourage small local screenings and sing-along nights with guidelines, printable assets, and simple contact paths for venues.

  • • Provide event tips and basic technical checklists.
  • • Offer artwork for posters and social media posts.

Merch & extra content

Keep a small, focused catalog of items that feel directly tied to the world of the show—no random add-ons, just things the characters might actually own.

  • • T-shirt, comic / book, and key artwork.
  • • Periodic refresh of designs, not constant churn.

Roadmap from the villain’s notebook.

The plan is written like any good lab experiment: start small, gather data, and adjust before building bigger machines. Dates below are approximate and may shift as reality and budget demand.

Phase I – Foundation

Keep the core musical easy to find.

Year 1

  • • Refresh official website and navigation.
  • • Consolidate links to streaming and purchase options.
  • • Publish clear lyrics & liner notes index.
  • • Release basic event guidelines for sing-alongs.
  • • Establish contact channel for venues and fan groups.
  • • Collect early feedback on site usability.

Phase II – Community

Support the people who won’t stop singing.

Years 2–3

  • • Highlight fan-organised events on a simple calendar.
  • • Add a small gallery of approved photos and posters.
  • • Introduce occasional Q&A or commentary sessions.
  • • Expand Mushortio section with short written pieces and odd lab notes.
  • • Offer limited-run variants of existing merchandise.
  • • Improve accessibility notes for screenings.

Phase III – New experiments

Explore new stories without breaking the old ones.

Beyond

  • • Develop ideas for short audio pieces or mini-songs.
  • • Consider small-scale animated or comic continuations.
  • • Partner with select venues for recurring sing-along nights.
  • • Test new ways for fans to participate remotely.
  • • Review all plans yearly against fan interest and time.
  • • Keep the tone: funny, a little sad, and very musical.

Lab notes behind the scenes.

The plan is intentionally flexible. Technology, platforms, and fan habits change, and the musical works best when it can follow along without losing its strange little heart.

  • • Review streaming platforms and rights on a regular schedule.
  • • Protect the tone of the show more carefully than any specific marketing plan.
  • • Keep the website fast, readable, and friendly to new visitors who may only know one song.

For questions about events, licensing, or anything that feels bigger than a laundromat conversation, use the contact page and choose the option that best matches your question.
