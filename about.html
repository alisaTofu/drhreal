About the Musical

A Sing-Along Story
of Love, Science, and Failure.

Dr. Horrible’s Sing-Along Blog is a short musical about a would-be super-villain who just wants to join the big leagues, impress the girl at the laundromat, and defeat a smug, shiny hero. It was released online, sung loudly by fans, and adopted by anyone who ever rooted for the awkward science kid.

This page gives a quick look at the story, the characters, and the creative team behind the goggles.

Cult-favorite musical

What is Dr. Horrible’s Sing-Along Blog?

The story follows Billy, a shy, brilliant scientist who keeps a video blog as his villain alter ego, Dr. Horrible. He dreams of joining the Evil League of Evil, but real life keeps getting in the way—mostly in the form of laundry day and a smug superhero called Captain Hammer.

It was released as a three-part web series with original songs, comic timing, and a surprising emotional punch. Fans around the world learned the lyrics, sang along at home, and turned this small experiment into a cult classic.

  • • Short, punchy episodes built for the web.
  • • Original songs that mix comedy, villainy, and heartbreak.
  • • A loyal fanbase that still sings along years later.

Format

Originally released online in three acts, later collected on disc and digital platforms with extras, commentaries, and liner notes.

Themes

Ambition, failure, unrequited love, and the question of whether becoming “evil” is ever as simple as it sounds.

Behind the Lab Goggles.

Behind every chaotic sing-along villain are writers, composers, and directors who plotted every joke, every chord, and every emotional gut punch.

Writing & Story

The architect of the blog.

Balancing comedy and tragedy, the story takes a familiar superhero world and quietly tilts it toward the villain’s point of view.

Music & Lyrics

Songs that stick in your head.

From villain monologues to awkward love duets, the songs make the world feel big, strange, and painfully relatable.

Direction & Performance

A small show with big feelings.

Shot with a compact crew and a focus on character moments, every performance leans into sincerity and absurdity at once.

Meet the central trio.

At the heart of the story are three people who want very different things—and keep crashing into each other’s musical numbers.

Dr. Horrible (Billy)

A brilliant, nervous scientist who blogs about villainy, applies to the Evil League of Evil, and practices speeches he may never give in person.

Penny

A hopeful idealist who wants to make the world better, chats politely in the laundromat, and never realizes how much Billy rehearses every word.

Captain Hammer

The city’s self-declared hero. Strong, shiny, and very sure of himself, he treats real people like props in his own never-ending victory parade.
