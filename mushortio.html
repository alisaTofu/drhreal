Mushortio

Short, strange stories
between the songs.

Mushortio is a collection of out-of-the-way pieces from the world of Dr. Horrible’s Sing-Along Blog: lab notes that never made it to screen, quiet moments between scenes, and experiments that probably should have stayed in the notebook.

Each entry is designed to stand on its own. You can read one on a lunch break, between commutes, or while you wait for the next sing-along night to start.

A single short piece is highlighted at a time. Entries may change as new pages wander out of the lab drawers.

Story • Last updated: 18 April

“Laundry Day Calibration”

The washers at the Coin Op are not calibrated for evil. They are barely calibrated for socks. But with enough quarters and a stolen sensor coil, it turns out you can measure how much hope a person has left by how long they stare at the spinning glass.

The entry follows one late-night cycle as Dr. Horrible tries to perfect a device that reads emotional resonance from laundry machines, while a very particular customer keeps choosing the broken dryer on purpose.

How Mushortio fits into the plan.

These stories are deliberately small. They are meant to deepen the world without turning it into something unrecognisable. When longer projects appear, Mushortio is where their ideas usually start.

  • • Short enough to read in one sitting, long enough to matter.
  • • Grounded in existing canon, with room for new perspectives.
  • • Written to be adaptable into songs, comics, or commentary later.

When new Mushortio pieces go live, they are added here first and may later appear in printed collections, recordings, or behind-the scenes material.
